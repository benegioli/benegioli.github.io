People From Florence

Dante Alighieri
Dante Alighieri is considered the father of the Italian language, because of his monumental work The Divine Comedy , which is universally regarded as the greatest written texts in the italian culture, and one of the best literary masterpieces of the whole world. An important linguist, political theorist and philosopher, Dante spun within the human knowledge, profoundly marking the Italian literature of the following centuries as well as Western culture, so much that he was nicknamed the "Great Poet" or simply the "Poet".



Giovanni Boccaccio
Giovanni Boccaccio was one of the most important figures in the European literary scene of the fourteenth century. Some experts define him as the greatest European storyteller of his time, a versatile writer who amalgamated different literary tendencies and genres making them converge into original works, thanks to a creative activity carried out under the banner of experimentalism.



Filippo Brunelleschi
Filippo Brunelleschi is considered the first engineer and designer of the modern era; he was one of the first three great initiators of the Florentine Renaissance with Donatello and Masaccio. In particular, Brunelleschi was the point of reference for the other two and to him we owe the invention of a single point of escape perspective, or "linear centric perspective". After an apprenticeship as a goldsmith and a career as a sculptor he devoted himself mainly to architecture, building almost exclusively in Florence, both secular and religious buildings that made history. Among these stands out the dome of Santa Maria del Fiore, an engineering masterpiece built without the aid of traditional techniques such as the rib.



Pietro Cimabue
In Cimabue, there is a fundamental step in the transition from hieratic and idealized figures (of Byzantine tradition) to true subjects, endowed with humanity and emotions, which will be at the base of Italian and Western painting. He was a painter of unscrupulous innovation that even without ever detaching from the proper Byzantine ways, he took them to the extreme consequences, one step away from the renewal already pursued in sculpture by Nicola Pisano and then in painting by Giotto



Leonardo da Vinci
A man of genius and universal talent of the Renaissance, he fully embodied the spirit of his era, bringing it to the greatest forms of expression in the most disparate fields of art and knowledge. He dealt with architecture and sculpture, he was draftsman, treatise writer, set designer, anatomist, musician, designer and inventor. He is considered one of the greatest geniuses of humanity.
