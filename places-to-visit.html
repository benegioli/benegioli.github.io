The Uffizzi gallery

It is one of the most important museums of the whole world, becauseit has a great quantity of unique masterpieces. There is the most important existing collection of Raffaello and Botticelli, as well as fundamental groups of works by Giotto, Tiziano, Veronese, Tintoretto, Pontormo, Bronzino, Caravaggio, Dürer, Rubens and many other great artists. In Pitti Palace are concentrated paintings of the sixteenth and the Baroque but also from the nineteenth and twentieth century Italian

The Annunciation by Verocchio and Da Vinci



Botticelli's Primavera, which is Italian for "Spring".



Brunelleschi's Dome and Campanile di Giotto

This is one of the most beautiful and fascinating buildings of the worlds; skilled architects are still not sure exactly how it was built. It was the largest dome in the world and still remains the largest masonry dome ever built. Thanks to the fundamental importance that it has played for the subsequent development of architecture and modern conception of construction, it is still today the most important architectural work ever built in Europe since Roman times. Its size prevented the traditional constructive method with the help of cèntine, making sure that many hypotheses were formulated on the construction technique used.

Ponte Vecchio

Ponte Vecchio, or "The Old Bridge" in English doesn't look particularly beautiful from outside, but it has an astonishing collection of jewellery stores inside. It also is one of the symbols of the city of Florence and one of the most famous bridges in the world. Legend says that during WWII, when the Germans where bombing down the bridges, they decided to leave Ponte Vecchio intact, because it was too beautiful to be detroyed. They probably left it unharmed because it was too small for tanks to pass through anyway, but it's still an interesting story.

Santa Croce

The Basilica of Santa Croce in the homonymous square in Florence, is one of the largest Franciscan churches and one of the greatest achievements of Gothic in Italy, and has the rank of minor basilica. It is the burial place of some of the most illustrious Italian characters, such as Michelangelo Buonarroti, Galileo Galilei, Niccolò Machiavelli, Vittorio Alfieri, Ugo Foscolo, Gioacchino Rossini, and is therefore known as the Temple of the Glory.

Pitti Palace

Palazzo Pitti was the residence of the Grand Duchy of Tuscany, already inhabited by the Medici, the Habsburg-Lorraine and, after the Unification of Italy, by the Savoy. It is located in Piazza dei Pitti, in the Oltrarno area. Inside there is a museum complex made up of galleries and museums of different nature: the Galleria Palatina (the art gallery preserves masterpieces by Raphael, Titian) arranged according to the criteria of the 18th century picture gallery, the Royal Apartments, the Apartment of the Duchess of Aosta and the District of the Prince of Naples, the Gallery of modern art and other specialized museums.

Palazzo Vecchio

Palazzo Vecchio is located in Piazza della Signoria in Florence and is the seat of the Municipality. It represents the best synthesis of the fourteenth-century civil architecture of the city and is one of the most famous civic buildings in the world. Originally called "Palazzo dei Priori", in the 15th century it became "Palazzo della Signoria", named after the main organism of the Republic of Florence; in 1540 it became a Doge's Palace, when Duke Cosimo I de 'Medici made it his residence; finally the name Old took it after 1565 when the court of Duke Cosimo moved into the "new" Palazzo Pitti. The best thing to visit the is the Salone dei Cinquecento (The Hall of the Five Hundred). It has amazing paintings and is really golden and cool.

Battistero di San Giovanni

The baptistery of San Giovanni Battista stands in front of the Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore, in Piazza San Giovanni. Dedicated to the patron saint of the city of Florence, it has the dignity of a minor basilica. The main facade of the octagonal building faces east, towards the dome, while the apse faces west. The Florentine baptistery was a place of investiture of knights and poets, as Dante Alighieri recalls in Paradise (XXV, 7-9): "con altra voce omai, con altro vello / ritornerò poeta, e in sul fonte / del mio battesmo prenderò 'l cappello". It was the seat for solemn oaths, as well as for the celebration in honor of the city patron with the gift of fine fabrics.

Piazza della Signoria

Piazza della Signoria is the central square of Florence, the seat of civil power and the heart of the social life of the city. L-shaped, it is located in the central part of medieval Florence, south of the Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore. In the past it has taken on different names, such as Piazza dei Priori or Piazza del Granduca.

Piazzale Michelangelo

Piazzale Michelangelo in Florence is the most famous observation point of the city scene, reproduced in countless postcards and obliged destination of tourists visiting the city. It's a very romantic place, and there are many beautiful gardens to visit and relax in, like The Roses Garden or the Iris garden (Florence's actual flower) In addition to this, a copy of Michelangelo's David is there.
